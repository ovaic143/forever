About CHNM trade

Computer Hardware & Network Maintenance (CHNM) at Govt ITI Anantnag builds your base in hardware, operating systems, and small network setup through structured theory plus intensive lab work.

Course structure

  • Duration: 1 year (2 semesters)
  • Pattern: Theory + lab-based practicals
  • Intake: 24 trainees (one unit, one shift)
  • Level: Aligned with NSQF

Outcomes

  • Profile: Hardware support, lab assistant, field technician
  • Skills: PC maintenance, LAN basics, OS support
  • Scope: Further specialization in networking, system administration

Inside the CHNM trade

A deeper view of what students actually do in the lab and classroom during the one-year journey.

Hardware foundation

Trainees learn to identify PC components, assemble systems, handle BIOS/UEFI settings, manage storage devices, and apply safe troubleshooting practices for common faults.

Operating systems

Installation and configuration of popular desktop operating systems, user and disk management, drivers, basic security, and handling common OS errors in lab environments.

Networking basics

Fundamentals of LAN, IP addressing, configuring simple network setups, testing connectivity, and understanding small office/lab network layouts.

Documentation & discipline

Students maintain proper practical notebooks, record faults and solutions, and follow lab discipline: ESD precautions, tool care, and standard procedures before and after every practical.

Soft skills & growth

Through viva, demonstrations and teamwork, trainees learn how to present faults, explain solutions, and prepare for real-world IT support communication.

Our projects

Sample lab activities and mini-projects that CHNM students typically work on during the batch.

PC assembly & health check

Complete desktop assembly from components, cable management, BIOS configuration, temperature and health checks, plus stress testing to ensure stable operation.

Small office LAN setup

Designing and setting up a small LAN with switches, assigning IPs, verifying connectivity and basic sharing between systems in the lab.

Dual-boot & OS recovery

Installing dual OS, managing partitions, repairing boot issues and backing up user data in case of system failure.

Trade overview â€“ CHNM

The CHNM trade focuses on building practical skills in assembling, configuring and maintaining personal computers and small networks. Trainees work on real systems under guidance, facing real faults instead of only theory examples.

By the end of the year, students should be confident in installing operating systems, solving common hardware issues, setting up small LANs and supporting basic IT needs in labs and offices.

Teaching philosophy â€“ Mr. Zahoor

Mr. Zahoor believes that learning hardware and networking requires repeated practice, clear steps and understanding the reason behind every action. His classes combine blackboard explanations with live demonstration on actual systems.

Students are expected to keep their practical records neat, follow safety in the lab and be honest about what they know and do not know so that doubts can be cleared properly.
